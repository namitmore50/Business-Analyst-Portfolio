# Executive Recommendations

## Purpose

This document provides executive-level recommendations derived from workforce analytics, statistical findings, SQL analysis, and dashboard insights.

The recommendations are designed to reduce employee attrition, improve workforce planning, strengthen retention, and reduce workforce-related financial risk.

---

# Recommendation 1 — Reduce Overtime Dependency

## Finding

Statistical analysis found a **strong relationship between overtime and employee attrition**.

### Key Evidence
- Employees working overtime have an attrition rate of **30.5%**
- Employees not working overtime show only **10.4% attrition**
- Employees working overtime are approximately **3× more likely to leave**
- **Chi-Square Test:** Significant relationship found (**p-value = 0.0000**)

---

## Business Impact

Excessive overtime contributes to:

- Employee burnout
- Lower morale
- Work-life imbalance
- Increased workforce instability
- Higher replacement cost

---

## Recommended Action

Leadership should:

- Monitor overtime-heavy departments
- Implement workload balancing
- Improve staffing in high-pressure teams
- Introduce employee wellness initiatives
- Reduce overtime dependency

---

## Estimated ROI

### Potential Outcome
Reducing overtime-related attrition by **5%** could significantly reduce workforce replacement costs and improve workforce stability.

### Expected Benefits
- Lower employee turnover
- Reduced hiring cost
- Improved employee wellbeing
- Higher productivity

# Recommendation 2 — Strengthen Early Employee Retention

## Finding

Employees with shorter tenure show higher attrition risk.

### Key Evidence
- Employees with **<1 year tenure** represent the highest-risk workforce segment.
- **Pearson Correlation (YearsAtCompany vs Attrition):**
  - **r = -0.1344**
  - Negative relationship found

---

## Business Impact

Early employee exits create:

- Increased hiring costs
- Onboarding waste
- Productivity loss
- Workforce instability

---

## Recommended Action

Leadership should:

- Strengthen onboarding programs
- Launch mentorship initiatives
- Improve first-year employee engagement
- Create structured career growth pathways
- Conduct regular check-ins for new employees

---

## Estimated ROI

Improving first-year retention may:

- Reduce avoidable resignations
- Lower replacement cost
- Improve workforce continuity
- Increase employee engagement

---

# Recommendation 3 — Improve Employee Satisfaction & Engagement

### Key Evidence

  - Negative relationship found
- Employees with lower satisfaction show higher exit tendency.

---

## Business Impact

Low employee satisfaction affects:

- Employee retention
- Productivity
- Team morale
- Organizational culture

---

## Recommended Action

Leadership should:

- Improve manager feedback systems
- Increase employee recognition
- Conduct engagement surveys
- Strengthen internal career growth
- Improve workplace experience

---

## Estimated ROI

Higher employee satisfaction may result in:

- Better retention
- Higher employee productivity
- Improved workforce morale
- Reduced recruitment cost

# Recommendation 4 — Improve Compensation Competitiveness

## Finding

Lower-paid employee segments demonstrate higher attrition tendency.

### Key Evidence
- **Pearson Correlation (Monthly Income vs Attrition):**
  - **r = -0.1598**
  - Negative relationship found
- Lower salary employees experience higher workforce instability.

---

## Business Impact

Poor compensation competitiveness increases:

- Employee exits
- External job switching
- Workforce dissatisfaction

---

## Recommended Action

Leadership should:

- Benchmark salary against market median
- Review compensation bands
- Improve internal growth opportunities
- Strengthen non-monetary employee benefits

---

## Estimated ROI

Competitive compensation may:

- Improve employee retention
- Reduce attrition cost
- Improve workforce satisfaction
- Reduce hiring dependency

# Executive Conclusion

The analysis identified **overtime workload, employee satisfaction, tenure, and compensation competitiveness** as major workforce attrition drivers.

Leadership should prioritize:

1. **Reducing overtime burden**
2. **Strengthening early employee retention**
3. **Improving employee satisfaction**
4. **Enhancing compensation competitiveness**

A proactive workforce intelligence strategy can significantly improve retention, reduce workforce cost, and strengthen long-term organizational stability.
## Key Takeaways

- Overtime is the strongest attrition signal
- Early tenure employees are highest risk
- Employee satisfaction impacts retention
- Compensation competitiveness matters
- Attrition has major financial cost
