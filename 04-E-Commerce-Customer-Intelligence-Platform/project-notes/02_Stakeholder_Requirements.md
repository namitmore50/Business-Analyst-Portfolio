## Stakeholder Power–Interest Matrix Description

Stakeholders were categorized using a Power–Interest Matrix to prioritize engagement and reporting frequency.

### High Power — High Interest (Manage Closely)

These stakeholders require regular reporting and active engagement:

* CEO
* COO
* CFO

Reason:
These stakeholders directly influence strategic decision-making, budgeting, and operational priorities.

---

### High Interest — Medium Power (Keep Engaged)

Stakeholders requiring detailed operational insights:

* CMO
* CRM Manager
* Operations Manager

Reason:
These teams depend on dashboard outputs for tactical business actions.

---

### Medium Interest — Medium Power (Keep Informed)

Stakeholders requiring periodic reporting:

* Merchandising Team
* Data Analysts

Reason:
These teams support business operations but do not directly drive strategic priorities.

## Stakeholder Engagement Strategy

The communication framework ensures stakeholders receive the right level of insight at the appropriate cadence.

Key principles followed:

1. Executive stakeholders receive summarized KPI-driven reporting.

2. Operational teams receive detailed drill-down analysis.

3. Marketing and CRM stakeholders receive segmentation and retention insights.

4. Reporting frequency aligns with business decision cycles.
